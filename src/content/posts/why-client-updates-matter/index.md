Finished work is only part of the story. A lot of what makes custom furniture interesting happens earlier, while the slab selection, pour planning, joinery decisions, and finish tests are still taking shape.

That is why this site is getting a journal feed instead of waiting until every post can be a full portfolio case study.

:::tip Why this is useful
Short updates make it easier to document the craft honestly and give clients a clearer sense of how a piece develops before it is done.
:::

Over time, that should make the site more useful in two ways:

1. It creates a record of process decisions that lead to better finished work.
2. It gives potential clients a better sense of how projects evolve in the shop.

Some posts will be polished write-ups. Some will just be clean, direct notes from the bench.

If there's anything you'd like to see more of, or if you have questions about the process, please let me know! I'm excited to share more of the shop's work as it happens.
